USMC Gear Borrowing Information:

USMC allows for it's members to borrow certain pieces of gear. You will be required to sign a liability form and demonstrate a specified level of skill which varies between each piece of gear.

Borrowable Gear





To borrow Sport climbing Gear you will need to show that you can tie the correct knots, put on a harness correctly and that you are able to belay another climber safely.

One of the most important peices of information you need to know is how to tie the Trace-Eight (or Trace Figure-Eight Knot), this is the most commonly used knot in climbing and is the knot you use to tie into a harness before you climb.

Being able to put on and tie into a harness correctly is another skill that is required in order to be able to climb safely, The club uses harnesses with 2 loops with a single one that goes between them which you will mostly be using and what the following video shows you how to tie into however the club also has absailing harnesses which differ because they only have a horizontal single loop. The difference when setting up for a climb with this harness is simply that you rope when you are the climber will only go through one loop instead of 2, when belaying you will need to becarful as due to the horizontal loop, it means that you belay will try to turn to the side so you will need to pay atention and try and keep the belay vertical to the best of your abillity.



Another important skill is belaying, The method that the BMC reccomends is in the Video bellow, some key points are that the teeth of the belay device should be pointing downwards, the belay device should be put onto the single vertical loop on a harness, there is an image on the side of most belay devices that will show a climber, that climber should be pointing upwards. The bite of rope that you put into the belay device should go through the hole which corisponds to what hand you are i.e right hole if right handed and vice versa. the gate of the carabiner should be facing the oposite direction to what hand you are, ie left if your right handed, the smaller end of a carabiner should also be towards your body.

Ettiquet is a big part of climbing, respecting and comunicating with your fellow climbers will lead to a better climbing expirence for all, it is important when you are out climbing that you communicate and make sure everyone is on the same page AND is comfertable with what ever you are doing. Keeping the location where you are climbing clean aswell is an important part of being a rock climber and insures that others will be able to use the rocks. Common ettiquet for when your climbing is that if someone is climbing a route that crosses over yours, you should wait for them to finish before you climb, if someone is trying to get down from somewhere and they are above you, you should wait for them to get down as they might not feel safe decending if your in the way due to the risk of accidently dropping something on you.
