USMC Gear Borrowing Information:

USMC allows for it's members to borrow certain pieces of gear. You will be required to sign a liability form and demonstrate a specified level of skill which varies between each piece of gear.

Gear Borrowing Form

This is the form you will need to complete. After completing the form you can either email it to example@surrey.ac.uk or print out and physically give the form to "whoever we decide will manage it".

Safety and skills requirments

This section will go over saftey around each of the equipment thats going to be borrowed and the skills that you have to demonstrate so that you may be allowed to borrow gear from the club.

Bouldering

To borrow boulder equipment you will need to demonstrate that you are able to spot a climber safely and that you have knowlage on where the best place to put boulder matts is on a boulder route. The following links will explain these 2 concepts in more detail.

USMC also follows a no trace left mentallity when using any public areas which most outdoor boulder problems will be located in, as such we expect all members that will be using club gear to treat the areas where you boulder with high amounts of respect. The following link will explain some basic outdoor bouldering concepts on how to help maintain the condition of where ever you are climbing.

Sport Climbing





To borrow Sport climbing Gear you will need to show that you can tie the correct knots, put on a harness correctly and that you are able to belay another climber safely.

One of the most important peices of information you need to know is how to tie the Trace-Eight (or Trace Figure-Eight Knot), this is the most commonly used knot in climbing and is the knot you use to tie into a harness before you climb.
